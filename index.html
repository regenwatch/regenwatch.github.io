REGENERATION WATCH

Monitoring council estate regeneration in London



Regeneration has consequences.

Research has shown the devastating impact of regeneration on council estate communities, often as a result of initial promises to residents not being kept.

During the last 5 years, Sadiq Khan has approved the demolition of over 7,000 council homes in 25 London Council estates.


Gascoigne Estate (west), Barking & Dagenham

Provides just 42% affordable housing and the definition of London Affordable Rent in the section 106 agreement allows rents of up to 80% market rent. No right to return for tenants or leaseholders.

Sebastian Court, Barking & Dagenham

59 council homes being replaced by 95 of which 29 are London Affordable Rent. No right to return for tenants or leaseholders.

Westhorpe Gardens & Mills Grove estate, Barnet

Arthur Street estate, Bexley


262 council homes to be demolished with a net loss of 134 social rent. No right of return for tenants or leaseholders.

Despite this, many boroughs are continuing to pursue demolition of estates based on claims that homes are hard to let or that design encourages crime/antisocial behaviour.
