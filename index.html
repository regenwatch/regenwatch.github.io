ESTATE REGENERATION PORTAL

Monitoring the regeneration of London's council estates


Regeneration has consequences.

Research has shown the devastating impact of regeneration on council estate communities, often as a result of initial promises not being kept.

Sadiq Khan has sought to redress this by introducing new policies for estate regeneration, requiring:

  • 1. At least 50% affordable housing with no net loss of social rent.
  • 2. A full ballot of residents before proceeding with demolition.
  • 3. A right to return to the estate for leaseholders and freeholders.
  • 4. A late stage viability review/affordable housing clawback
  • 5. Demolition to be considered only as a last resort

But the Mayor is turning a blind eye to an increasing number of schemes that fail these requirements:



Gascoigne Estate (west), Barking & Dagenham

Only 42% affordable with the definition of 'London Affordable Rent' allowing rents of up to 80% market rent. No viability review, no right to return for tenants or leaseholders and residents denied a ballot.

High Path estate, Merton


608 homes being replaced by 1,570 of which only 277 affordable (affordable rent > 80% market rent). Residents denied a ballot.

Eastfields estate, Merton


466 homes being replaced by 800 of which only 262 affordable (affordable rent > 80% market rent). Residents denied a ballot.

Sebastian Court, Barking & Dagenham


59 council homes being replaced by 95 of which 29 are London Affordable Rent. No right to return for tenants or leaseholders.

Westhorpe Gardens & Mills Grove estate, Barnet

Arthur Street estate, Bexley


262 council homes to be demolished with a net loss of 134 social rent. No right of return for tenants or leaseholders.

Pike Close estate, Bromley


The Mayor's new policy also says that demolition should only be pursued as a last resort and that any demolition proposal should assess the "wider social and environmental impacts. Despite this, many boroughs are continuing to pursue full redevelopment on tenuous claims that homes are hard to let or that design encourages crime/antisocial behaviour.

Five London boroughs pursuing such an approach are shown on our watch list below - click on the links to see the estates earmarked for redevelopment in each borough.
