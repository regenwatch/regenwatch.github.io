Estates under threat

An increasing number of London's council estates are being earmarked for redevelopment. We're monitoring to ensure that schemes comply with the Mayor's new policy requirements of:

  • At least 50% affordable housing with no net loss of social rent.
  • A fair deal and right to return for residents.
  • That demolition is considered only as a last resort

Retrofirst: re-use & re-purpose

Refurbishment is nearly always the best option

Research has shown that refurbishment is nearly always the best option when environmental and social factors are properly taken into account.
  • Economically

    Redevelopment appraisals routinely fail to take into account knock-on costs like increased temporaray accommodation costs or lost rental income.

  • Socially

    Redevelopment appraisals rarely take into account the social cost of splitting up long-standing communities or the knock-on effects on the housing waiting list.

  • Environmentally

    Redevelopment appraisals rarely take into account the thousands of tonnes of embodied carbon in existing buildings or the carbon footprint of their demolition and replacement.
