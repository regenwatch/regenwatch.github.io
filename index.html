Regeneration has consequences.

Research has shown the devastating impact of regeneration on council estate communities, often as a result of initial promises to residents not being kept.

During the last 5 years, Sadiq Khan has approved the demolition of over 7,000 council homes in 25 London Council estates.



The Mayor's own policy says that demolition of council estates should only be approved as a last resort i.e. where there are structural issues that cannot be resolved. Despite this, many boroughs are continuing to pursue demolition of estates based on claims that homes are hard to let or that design encourages crime/antisocial behaviour.
