ESTATE REGENERATION PORTAL

Monitoring the regeneration of London's council estates


Regeneration has consequences.

Research has shown the devastating impact of regeneration on council estate communities, often as a result of initial promises not being kept.

Sadiq Khan has approved the demolition of ** council estates since he was elected in May 2016, many of which he has provided funding for and exempted from his own requirement to ballot residents on the demolition of their homes:



Gascoigne Estate (west), Barking & Dagenham

Received Mayor's final approval in Dec 2018. The definition of 'London Affordable Rent' allowing rents of up to 80% market rent - scheme exempted from ballot requirement.

High Path estate, Merton


In Nov 2018, the Mayor approved the demolition of 608 homes replaced by 1,570 of which only 20% affordable (affordable rent > 80% market rent). Scheme funded by Mayor and exempted from ballot requirement.

Eastfields estate, Merton


In Nov 2018, the Mayor approved demolition of 466 homes replaced by 800 of which only 262 affordable (affordable rent > 80% market rent). Scheme funded by Mayor and exempted from ballot.

Becontree Heath, Barking & Dagenham


Received Mayor's final approval in Feb 2018. Scheme funded by Mayor and exempted from ballot requirement.

The Mayor's policies also require demolition to be pursued as a last resort and that any demolition proposal should assess the "wider social and environmental impacts. Despite this, many boroughs are continuing to pursue full redevelopment on tenuous claims that estates are hard to let or that design encourages crime/antisocial behaviour.
