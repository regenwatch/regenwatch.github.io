  • Sadiq Khan's new regeneration policy

    In response to several high-profile and controversial estate regeneration schemes, in 2018 the Mayor introduced new policies requiring schemes to provide at least 50% affordable housing with no net loss of social rented and a full ballot of residents. The following schemes fail to comply with these requirements and the Mayor has taken no action:

    Wandsworth Council's redevelopment of the Winstanley/York Rd estate fails to meet the 50% affordable housing requirement, has failed to apply for grant funding and has failed to conduct a ballot.


    The same goes for Westminster Council's redevelopment of the Ebury Bridge estate.

    We say the Mayor should use his planning powers to 'call in' and take control of planning decisions on these schemes to ensure they comply with his policy requirements

    .

  • Residents Charter

    Past experience has shown that when estate regeneration is properly considered, refurbishment is almost always the most viable option. Proposals often fail to take policy requirements or residents rehousing into account. Make sure that these are protected from the outset and enshrined in a Residents Charter, using our template
