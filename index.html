REGENERATION WATCH

Monitoring council estate regeneration in London

Regeneration has consequences.

Research has shown the devastating impact of regeneration on council estate communities, often as a result of initial promises not being kept.

The Mayor has sought to redress these issues by introducing new policies for estate regeneration, requiring:

  • 1. At least 50% affordable housing with no net loss of social rent.
  • 2. A full ballot of residents before proceeding with demolition.
  • 3. A right to return to the estate for leaseholders and freeholders.
  • 4. A late stage viability review/affordable housing clawback

But the Mayor is turning a blind eye to an increasing number of schemes that fail to meet these requirements:



Gascoigne Estate (west) regeneration, Barking & Dagenham

Reprovision of 42% affordable housing with the definition of 'London Affordable Rent' allowing rents of up to 80% market rent. No viability review or right to return for tenants or leaseholders.

Sebastian Court, Barking & Dagenham


59 council homes being replaced by 95 of which 29 are London Affordable Rent. No right to return for tenants or leaseholders.

Westhorpe Gardens & Mills Grove estate, Barnet

Arthur Street estate, Bexley


262 council homes to be demolished with a net loss of 134 social rent. No right of return for tenants or leaseholders.

The Mayor's own policy says that demolition of council estates should only be approved as a last resort i.e. where there are structural issues that cannot be resolved. Despite this, many boroughs are continuing to pursue demolition of estates based on claims that homes are hard to let or that design encourages crime/antisocial behaviour.
