Funding

The obligation to rehouse tenants and homeowners in estate regeneration schemes is enshrined within the general legal _“principle of equivalence”_ which says that persons affected by compulsory purchase should be no worse off in financial terms after the compulsory acquisition and section 233(paras 5 & 7) of the Town and Country Planning Act 1990, which says that persons compulsorily removed from their homes for planning purposes must be offered 'suitable accommodation' on the redeveloped land _'on terms settled with due regard to the price at which any such land has been acquired from him'_. Despite this legal obligation, all that the Mayor's Estate Regeneration Policy says is that tenants and leaseholders must be offered a 'fair deal'
