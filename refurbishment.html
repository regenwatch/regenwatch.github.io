Making the case for refurbishment

Research has shown that refurbishment of council estates is nearly always the best option when taking into account social and environtmental factors. Refurbishment can deliver significant improvements in energy, environmental and health performance, which can lead to costs savings and improved living standards for residents. It can have lower overall lifetime costs than redevelopment and can cause less disruption to communities.

Demolition - doubly wasteful.

Refurbishment - better environmentally & socially.

University College London has produced a detailed report outlining the merits of refurbishment versus demolition. The Mayor's new Estate Regeneration Guidance says that demolition should only be considered as a last resort and he recently introduced new Circular Economy Guidance. Case studies have been supported by a 'retrofit' campaign led by the Architects' Journal.

Economically - the business case.

Option appraisals often fail to take a number of crucial factors into account:

  • The financial cost of decanting the estate including buy-out costs of leaseholders.
  • The loss of rental income for the Council both in the short term and long term.
  • Increased temporary accommodation costs as a result of decanting tenants into existing stock.
Our template Residents' Charter sets out detailed requirements ensuring that theseconsiderations as well as environmental factors are taken into account from the outset.
