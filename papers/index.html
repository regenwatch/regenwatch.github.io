The Research

Over the last 3 years, the University of Leicester and King’s College London have undertaken a research project that has provided detailed evidence since 1997 of the displacement of London council tenants and leaseholders through regeneration schemes. As part of this research, 120 in-depth interviews were carried out with residents on 6 different council estates undergoing regeneration.

This article contains an analysis of the numerical data on the 161 estate researched since 1997. The article highlights the researchers' difficulties in finding reliable data to measure displacement and the subsequent reliance on a mixture of proxy measures. The latter can reveal tendencies but cannot distinguish between involuntary displacement from council estates, voluntary outmigration or incumbent socio-economic uplift.
