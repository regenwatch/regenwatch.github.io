--- name: Carpenters estate layout: estate images: - image_path: /images/carpenters.jpg - image_path: /images/carpenters1.jpg - image_path: /images/carpenters2.jpg - image_path: /images/carpenters3.jpg --- The Carpenters Estate was completed in 1970, providing 710 homes. Plans for its regeneration have been under consideration since 2000, with several buildings earmarked for demolition and the decanting of residents having started in 2005. The majority of council tenants have been moved out and mostly leaseholders and freeholders remain. The estate has several active pieces of legislation to ensure that the residents have control in the estate's future, including the Carpenters Estate Residents’ Charter and the Greater Carpenters Neighbourhood Plan. In February 2020 the Council gave approval for Red Door Ventures to work with the residents on a development plan for the estate.

Key themes discussed in the interviews:

  • The estate’s regeneration has been in development for many years with residents describing it as having gone on for a long time with very little resolve in sight.
  • Some residents have been moved out and several buildings remain empty, creating a ‘ghost town’ and circulating a feeling that the estate has been intentionally depopulated and degenerated to favour regeneration.
  • Whilst some residents have moved away, there has been a long term resistance to the estate’s regeneration. Keeping the community together is a common priority.
  • The area is experiencing a changing social character, in particular the new-build homes that border the estate are described as being for foreign investors not locals.
  • The Olympic Games and the Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park, Westfield Shopping centre, Stratford International and other train links all add to gentrification pressure on the estate.

Freeholder

"The grass was overgrown, boarded-up buildings, and I thought this is what they want the world to see. Do you know what I mean? Because one day, we were walking out, and I walked out that way, to the back, and that small block, it was all, it was almost like it was a ghost town. All of these boarded-up buildings, and I thought, this is, and I walked around, you saw more and more and it was like, where am I living? It’s just wasn’t the same place, and your heart sunk. Your heart just sunk it was like, this isn’t the Carpenters Estate anymore. But that is what they want you to feel."

Leaseholder

"… And then they started decanting people. It felt like, of course very upsetting [sic]. Because, my plan was to, you know, I am a leaseholder, so, while I was working I purchased the flat, and slowly tried to pay it off. And then have this as my retirement flat, and live here. But all of that has been disturbed. And the process was quite bad, they were trying to knock at the door without any notification. They will just drop the card, saying ‘Can you contact us’?
